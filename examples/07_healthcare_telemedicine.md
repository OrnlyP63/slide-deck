## 1. Clinical and Access Context

### Rural Americans die 3.4 years younger than urban peers — access gap is primary driver
**Situation:** 46 million Americans live in rural areas with physician-to-population ratios 60% below urban average; 1,800 rural hospitals have closed since 2010 leaving 30M with no local specialist access
**Complication:** COVID-era telemedicine waivers created a natural experiment demonstrating 35% reduction in preventable hospitalisation, but permanent reimbursement parity remains unresolved in 31 states
**Resolution:** Analysis of 47-county longitudinal cohort (n=284,000) demonstrates telemedicine parity reimbursement generates $4,200 per-patient annual savings while reducing 30-day readmission by 28%
> Source: Rural Health Research Gateway 2025; CMS Telemedicine Data 2020–2025; Our cohort study

### Four structural barriers prevent rural patients from accessing specialist care
- Distance: median 47-mile drive to nearest specialist; 31% lack reliable transport
- Workforce: rural areas have 1 primary care physician per 3,500 residents vs 1:1,200 urban
- Digital access: 21% of rural households lack broadband sufficient for video consultation
- Insurance: 38% of rural adults are uninsured or underinsured vs 14% urban
> Source: HRSA Rural Health Report 2025; FCC Broadband Data Collection; KFF Health Insurance Survey

## 2. Study Findings

### Three numbers summarise five years of cohort evidence
**Preventable hospitalisations reduced:** 35%
**Per-patient annual savings:** $4,200
**Mental health access increase:** 3.8×
> Source: CMS claims data; State health records; Our analysis. IRB approved. n=284,000

### Telemedicine impact strongest for chronic disease management and mental health
#### Conditions with Greatest Impact
- Diabetes management: HbA1c control improved 22%, hospitalisation -41%
- Hypertension: uncontrolled BP prevalence fell 31%
- Mental health: treatment access increased 3.8× in counties with no local psychiatrist
#### Access Gains by Population
- Patients over 65: 67% increase in specialist consultations
- Uninsured: 44% increase in preventive care visits when zero-cost telemedicine offered
- Spanish-speaking: 2.9× increase with Spanish-language platform availability

## 3. Policy Recommendations

### Access to healthcare should not depend on zip code
> "Of all the forms of inequality, injustice in health care is the most shocking and inhumane."
> — Martin Luther King Jr., 1966

### Four interventions close the rural health gap — costs negligible vs benefits
| Intervention | Federal Cost/yr | Projected Saving/yr | Net |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reimbursement parity | $2.3B | $11.4B | +$9.1B |
| Rural broadband | $1.5B (one-time) | $2.1B | +$0.6B |
| Licensure compact | $45M | $780M | +$735M |
| Device programme | $340M | $2.1B | +$1.76B |
> Source: Our economic model; CBO scoring methodology; Published telemedicine literature

### Policy implementation: three steps in 18 months
1. Months 1–6: Congress passes permanent reimbursement parity; CMS issues guidance
2. Months 7–12: FCC rural broadband deployment accelerated; interstate licensure compact expanded
3. Months 13–18: Federal device programme launches; 30M rural patients covered
> Source: Legislative timeline; CMS implementation capacity assessment

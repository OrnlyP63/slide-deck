## 1. Background and Research Questions

### Microplastic contamination has reached every freshwater ecosystem studied since 2015
**Situation:** Microplastics (<5mm) detected in 100% of freshwater sites studied across 42 countries; average concentration increased 4.7× from 2015 to 2024
**Complication:** Current regulatory frameworks address marine plastics only; freshwater pathways into drinking water and food chains remain unregulated and poorly quantified
**Resolution:** Ten-year longitudinal dataset across 847 monitoring sites enables first dose-response model linking freshwater microplastic concentration to aquatic biodiversity loss
> Source: Our field study 2015–2024; UNEP Freshwater Plastics Assessment 2024; WHO Microplastics in Drinking Water 2022

### Three mechanisms drive freshwater microplastic accumulation at accelerating rates
- Atmospheric deposition contributes 32% of freshwater load — significantly underestimated before 2020
- Wastewater treatment removes only 72% of microplastics; effluent is primary point source
- Agricultural runoff containing microplastic-coated fertiliser granules emerging as secondary source
- Fragmentation of larger plastic debris in-stream generates secondary microplastic at rates non-linear with temperature
> Source: Leads et al. 2022; Zhang et al. 2023; Our field data 2015–2024

### Biodiversity impacts emerge at concentrations 40× lower than current OECD guidelines
- Macroinvertebrate species richness declines 18% at 1,500 particles/L (OECD threshold: 60,000)
- Fish larval development impaired at 800 particles/L in controlled mesocosm experiments
- Bioaccumulation factor of 340× measured from water column to apex predator tissue
- No-observable-effect concentration identified at 200 particles/L for sensitive species
> Source: Our mesocosm experiments 2021–2024; Rochman et al. 2023; Peer-reviewed meta-analysis n=67 studies

## 3. Policy Implications

### Current regulatory thresholds are 40× too permissive based on dose-response evidence
| Jurisdiction | Current limit | Evidence-based limit | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| OECD guideline | 60,000 particles/L | 500 particles/L | 120× |
| EU Water Framework | None | 500 particles/L | No standard |
| US EPA | Monitoring only | 500 particles/L | No limit |
> Source: Our dose-response modelling; Regulatory database review

### Science demands a fundamental rethink of freshwater plastic governance
> "We do not inherit the Earth from our ancestors; we borrow it from our children."
> — Antoine de Saint-Exupery

### Three regulatory interventions would reduce freshwater microplastic load by 68%
1. Mandatory tertiary treatment at wastewater plants serving >50,000 population: 41% reduction
2. Extended producer responsibility for single-use plastics with freshwater proximity weighting: 18% reduction
3. Agricultural microplastic-free fertiliser standards by 2028: 9% reduction
> Source: Our modelling using field data and treatment efficacy literature; Cost-benefit analysis in supplementary materials
